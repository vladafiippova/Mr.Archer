Significant Volcanic Eruptions

General Information

A volcano is an opening in the Earth's crust that allows molten rock, gases, and debris to escape to the surface. Alaska, Hawaii, California, and Oregon have the most active volcanoes. A volcanic eruption may involve lava and other debris eruption that can flow up to 100 mph, destroying everything in their path. Volcanic ash travels 100s of miles and causes severe health problems, as we saw in Washington state this year.

Hazard Awareness

Volcanic eruptions are one of the world's most striking and hostile events. Their powerful explosive eruptions drastically change Earth's surface for tens of miles from the eye of the explosion, but also the atmosphere is impacted. Small molten droplets of sulfuric acid burst into the stratosphere, destroying the ozone layer and changing the atmosphere. Volcanoes made people frequently move and abandon their homes during the eruption. The U.S. Geological Survey closely monitors all U.S. volcanoes to enhance public safety and minimize the economic impact. Still, volcanoes are responsible for the destruction of electric grids, transportation systems, cities, and death due to the damage created by ash, lahars, and flooding.
